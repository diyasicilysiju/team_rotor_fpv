ABOUT US

Who We Are

Team Identity

Team Rotor FPV is a specialized drone development collective at VIT University, Vellore. We operate at the intersection of high-speed aerial cinematography and advanced robotic engineering.

Our work spans from designing ultra-light racing frames to developing autonomous flight algorithms for search and rescue simulations — pushing the boundaries of what university drone teams can achieve.

Where We Work

Workspace & Grounds

Operating out of GDN 129, our primary lab serves as a hub for UAS research. We maintain a full suite of rapid prototyping tools including CNC carbon cutters and precision soldering stations.

Flight testing occurs at SJT Ground and KC Lawn, providing the clearance necessary for high-Betaflight tuning and 6S power system stress tests.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

How can I join Rotor FPV?

Recruitments happen once a year, typically at the start of the academic term. We look for students passionate about embedded systems, structural design, aerodynamics, or professional piloting. No prior drone experience is required — just drive.

Do I need to own a drone to join?

Not at all. We provide all the equipment, simulators, and training required to take you from zero to a certified team pilot. Our lab is equipped with everything you need to learn, build, and fly.

What competitions does the team participate in?

We compete in national drone championships including the Indian Space Drone Championship (ISDC), where we secured 1st place in 2026. We also participate in inter-university UAV challenges and autonomous flight events.

Which departments can apply?

We welcome students from all engineering disciplines — mechanical, electrical, electronics, computer science, and even design. The team thrives on multi-disciplinary collaboration, and every skill set has a role here.
